Work Station Essentials

Overview

When setting up a PC workstation, itâ€™s always good to have ample room on the desk and underneath as well in order to organize cables in a clean manner. Here are steps you should follow when setting up your workstation.


1. Make sure you have enough surge protected outlets for the tower (term for the actual PC), monitor (screen used to interact with the CPU through the GUI, or Graphic User Interface), and any other hardware accessories such as a printer, fax, scanner, secondary monitor(s), etc. If you don't have enough, use a surge-protected power strip to ensure that in case of a thunderstorm or electrical surge the PC and any other devices are protected from electrical damage.

2. Connect the power cable to the PC, and the power and video cable (VGA, DVI, or HDMI) to the monitor.

3. Connect the monitor's video cable (VGA, HDMI, DVI, etc.) to the appropriate port on the back of the PC.

4. Connect the keyboard and mouse either via USB (wired or wireless) or PS/2 port.

5. Adjust the monitor angle, and the mouse and keyboard placement, to ensure comfort and reduce eye and wrist strain from long periods of use.

6. With the power strip connected to the outlet but turned off, connect the monitor and PC power cables.

7. Once all power connections are made, and the cables connected securely into the monitor and PC, turn on the power strip.

8. Ensure the monitor has power before pressing the power button on the PC.

9. After pressing the power button, let the PC boot up properly, following any on screen prompts if the PC is a recent purchase for first time set up.



Congratulations, you have successfully set up your PC workstation. Follow the directions and/or installation CDs included with any scanners, printers, or other hardware that will be connected to the PC. Make sure there is enough space around the PC to allow maximum airflow to keep your PC cool and running.

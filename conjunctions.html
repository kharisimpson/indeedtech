Conjunctions

Overview

Conjunctions are extremely important to know because itâ€™s a huge part of grammar. You need to know how and why to use a conjunction, in order to make a proper sentence. Conjunctions are words used to connect clauses or sentences or to coordinate words in the same clause. While using conjunctions, you also need to know how to use the correct fanboys to complete the sentence. Fanboys join words, phrases, and clauses. There are three types of conjunctions: coordinating, correlating, and subordinating.



Coordinating Conjunctions are the FANBOYS that join words, phrases, and independent clauses.

Ex. The dog is happy inside the house, but he is happier outside the house.

In the example the FANBOY, but, is used to separate the two independent clauses, The dog is happy inside the house and he is happier outside the house.

Correlative Conjunctions are pairs of conjunctions that must be used together and create correlation between things.

Ex. either, or. Either you will go to the store or I will.

In the example, either, is used to separate the independent clause, you will go to the store and, or, relates the dependent clause I will back to the beginning of the sentence.

Subordinating Conjunctions creates dependent clauses and tie them to independent clauses.

Ex. Before you go, make sure you clean your room.

In the example, before, begins the dependent clause, you go, which ties back to the independent clause, make sure you clean your room.
